6. Communication ke Barriers

üîä "Noise" is anything that blocks or distorts a message

üîä

Physical Noise

External distractions: loud environments, poor lighting, bad connections, information overload

ü§í

Physiological Noise

Internal body issues: fatigue, illness, headaches, disabilities, intoxication

üß†

Psychological Noise

Mental/emotional states: anger, bias, anxiety, preconceptions, apathy

üè¢

Organizational Barriers

Rigid hierarchies, bureaucracy distorting information through multiple layers

üì±

Channel Barriers

Using ineffective medium for the message (wrong communication channel)

üåç

Cultural & Linguistic

Different languages, jargon, cultural norms affecting interpretation

üõ†Ô∏è Overcoming Communication Barriers:

Active Listening: Listen to understand, not just to respond
Clear Messaging: Use specific, unambiguous language
Choose Right Channel: Match medium to message type
Encourage Feedback: Create open communication climate

7. Communication Channels

üë•

Two-way, Face-to-Face

  • Features: Oral & non-verbal, immediate feedback, highly personal
  • Examples: One-to-one meetings, board meetings, presentations
  • Advantages: Non-verbal cues, immediate feedback, personal quality
  • Best for: Problem solving, relationship building
üìû

Two-way, Not Face-to-Face

  • Features: Purely oral, immediate feedback, impersonal
  • Examples: Telephone, video calls, teleconferencing
  • Advantages: Saves time/cost, reaches distant people
  • Best for: Quick discussions, remote coordination
üìù

One-way, Not Face-to-Face

  • Features: Written, no immediate feedback, formal
  • Examples: Letters, reports, memos, emails, notices
  • Advantages: Permanent record, detailed information
  • Best for: Complex ideas, formal tone, documentation
